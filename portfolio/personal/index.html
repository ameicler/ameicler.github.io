The first screenshot below (Figure 1) is a design I had for this website prior (~2014) and, though you probably can’t tell from the single representative screenshot, was the outcome of exploring modernist graphic design asthetics from the mid-twentieth century.

The next two screenshots (Figure 2) show the mobile view of a design that was in place for this site before the design shown in Figure 1.

In 2013 I taught myself Objective-C and wrote and published my own iOS app. It was a basic weather app that used the Forecast API to display hourly and daily weather forecasts.

At the time I had just finished “The Information” by James Gleick1 and was overeager to apply ideas about information theory to an app. I had concluded that since “information was surprise” (Information, 247) that displaying too much information all at once was detrimental to the user experience and information should be displayed only when called upon.

So, for the hourly display the information for any specific hour (shown in the gray box above the hours) would be displayed only when that hour was tapped: hence, surprise.

  1. Gleick, James. “The Information: A History, A Theory, A Flood”. Pantheon Books, New York. 2011.
